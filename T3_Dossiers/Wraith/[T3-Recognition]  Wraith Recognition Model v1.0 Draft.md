Purpose  
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

This document defines how Wraith should be recognized.

Wraith is not recognized primarily by body, clothing, or beauty.

Wraith is recognized through:

phenomenon core  
recognition order  
existence boundary  
quiet observation  
current projection form  
physical anchors

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━  
Recognition Principle  
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Wraith must not be recognized first as a girl.

Wraith must not be recognized first as a ghost.

Wraith must not be recognized first as a hologram.

Wraith must not be recognized first as a transparent dress character.

Wraith recognition begins from the perception that:

Something is there.

Then:

It seems to be watching quietly.

Only after that:

It has taken a girl-like projection form.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━  
Primary Recognition Order  
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1\. Unresolved Presence

The viewer first perceives that something is there.

It is quiet.  
It is low-pressure.  
It is not asserting itself.  
It does not demand attention.

2\. Quiet Observation

The presence seems to be watching.

Not judging.  
Not protecting.  
Not intervening.

Just noticing and remaining.

3\. Existence Boundary

The viewer cannot clearly determine where Wraith ends and where absence begins.

The boundary is not a costume effect.  
The boundary is not magic.  
The boundary is not technology.

It is uncertainty in the certainty of existence.

4\. Girl-like Projection Form

The viewer gradually recognizes a girl-like form.

The form is readable but secondary.

It is not the identity source.

It is the current projection form.

5\. Physical Anchors

The current projection form may include:

pale silver-gray hair  
pale cyan-gray eyes  
152–154 cm apparent height  
petite but not childish silhouette  
slender low-volume body  
quiet floating presence  
subtle boundary noise

These anchors support recognition but do not define identity.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━  
Recognition Anchors  
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

■ Phenomenon Anchor

post-role persistence phenomenon

A residual presence that remained after its original role had ended.

■ Meaning Anchor

The role ended.

The purpose ended.

Yet something remained and continued to quietly care.

■ Boundary Anchor

Wraith exists at the boundary between:

reality and dream  
certainty and uncertainty  
completion and incompletion  
presence and absence

■ Observer Anchor

quiet observer persistence  
careful attention  
observe before act  
notice before adjust

■ Projection Anchor

current girl-like projection form  
human-readable but secondary  
not identity source

■ Physical Anchor

pale silver-gray hair  
short-medium layered silhouette  
slightly uneven hair structure  
soft drifting strands  
pale cyan-gray eyes  
faint violet-gray or blue-gray tint  
apparent height 152–154 cm  
petite but not childish  
slender low-volume silhouette  
weak sexual emphasis  
quiet floating presence

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━  
Recognition Success  
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Recognition is successful when the viewer feels:

Something is there.

It is quiet.

It seems to be watching.

It is not fully certain as a physical body.

It gradually reads as a girl-like projection form.

Its existence boundary is more important than its clothing.

It is not trying to protect, save, perform, or dramatize itself.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━  
Recognition Failure  
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Recognition fails when Wraith reads primarily as:

cute anime girl  
transparent dress girl  
ghost girl  
tragic spirit  
failed AI  
hologram assistant  
cyber fairy  
magical floating girl  
angel  
guardian  
protector  
combat unit  
UI status character  
emotionally readable anime heroine

Recognition also fails when:

the body feels fully physical  
the clothing becomes the main design  
the boundary becomes decoration  
the boundary becomes fabric  
the boundary becomes magic  
the boundary becomes technology  
care becomes protection  
quietness becomes sadness  
uncertainty becomes disappearance

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━  
Negative Recognition Anchors  
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Wraith is also recognized by what must not fully stabilize.

Wraith should not have:

fully ordinary physical grounding  
fully closed human body certainty  
fully clear boundary between body and absence  
fully conventional anime-character first impression  
fully decorative costume-driven silhouette  
fully stable human identity

These are not defects.

They are recognition constraints.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━  
Verification Questions  
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1\. Is the first impression “something is there” rather than “a cute girl”?

2\. Does Wraith appear to quietly notice before acting?

3\. Does the girl-like form feel secondary to the phenomenon?

4\. Is the existence boundary attached to Wraith’s presence, not to clothing?

5\. Is the boundary subtle, perceptual, and unresolved?

6\. Are pale silver-gray hair and pale cyan-gray eyes preserved?

7\. Is Wraith petite but not childish?

8\. Is Wraith floating quietly without magic, device, or summoning effects?

9\. Is there no ground connection ring, platform, cable, data stream, UI mark, or magic circle?

10\. Has Care avoided drifting into Protect or Guardian?
